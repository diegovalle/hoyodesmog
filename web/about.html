About

A website to remind you the state of Mexico City's government is literally non-ironically poisoning you this instant

The World Air Quality Index (AQI) is brought to you by aqicn.org. The traditonal 0-500 AQI scale, based on the US EPA standard, is used for indexing the pollution. Air Quality Data is updated in real-time, every hour. Most of the Index corresponds to PM2.5 particule size measurement, with some exceptions based on PM10. Air quality in CDMX™ is recorded by the Sistema de Monitoreo Atmosférico de la Ciudad de México, the monitoring system maintained by the city environmental agency. Established in 1986, the network consists of monitoring stations distributed throughout Mexico City.6 The network reports hourly measures of carbon monoxide, nitrogen dioxide, ozone, nitrogen oxides, and sulfur dioxide.
